Gardening

Gardening is one of my newest hobbies but I have always valued plants in my environment. After moving to Philadelphia for my doctoral studies in 2021, I found that fresh produce is relatively expensive, so I decided to grow my own. First, I contacted the local student-managed gardening working group (Drexel Urban Growers) to learn the basics and I will continue to be an active member as much as I can find the time. Then I started building my first raised bed in the backyard. The first season, 2022, went poorly as my efforts were hampered by constructions at the neighbor's house and animal competitors. I'll try to focus more on native species next season.




Not only do I enjoy eating my plants, but I also find them very aesthetically pleasing with their amazing variety of shapes, colors and geometries. I especially like the plants in the hibiscus family. Here in Philadelphia I only have the one growing in my garden and one in my office, but in good old Germany there are several ones waiting for me to come back. My idea is to have a small collection of different varieties from around the world in the future. I also enjoy photographing trees, as well as other plants. Their organic growth forms are visually very appealing. Find some pictures here on my website or on my instagram.
